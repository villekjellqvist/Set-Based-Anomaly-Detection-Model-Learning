The posterior distribution of the measurements given the model parameters is
\begin{equation*}
    p(y_{[0:k]}\big{\vert}\theta, u_{[0:k]}) 
    = \prod\limits_{i=1}^k \mathcal{N}(y[i]\big{|}\varphi[i]^T\theta, \frac{\gamma^2}{\lambda^{k-i}})
\end{equation*}
where 
\begin{equation}
        \begin{split}
                \varphi[k]^T &= \begin{bmatrix}
                        y_{[k-1:k-n]} & u_{[k-1:k-n]}  
                \end{bmatrix} \\
                y_{[k-1:k-n]} &= \begin{bmatrix}
                        y[k-1] & y[k-2] & \cdots & y[k-n]
                \end{bmatrix} \\
                u_{[k-1:k-n]}  &= \begin{bmatrix}
                        u[k-1] & u[k-2] & \cdots & u[k-n]
                \end{bmatrix}
        \end{split}
\end{equation}
and $\lambda \in (0,1])$ is a forgetting factor.
Define 
\begin{equation}
\Sigma = \text{diag}\begin{bmatrix}
    \frac{\gamma^2}{\lambda^k} & \frac{\gamma^2}{\lambda^{k-1}} & \cdots &
    \frac{\gamma^2}{\lambda^0}
\end{bmatrix}
\end{equation}
and
\begin{equation}
    \Phi_{[0:k]} = \begin{bmatrix}
        \varphi[0] & \varphi[1] & \cdots & \varphi[k]
    \end{bmatrix}^T
\end{equation}
giving us the squared Mahalonobis distance $D_M$
\begin{equation}
    D_M = (y_{[0:k]} - \Phi_{[0:k]}\theta)^T\Sigma^{-1}(y_{[0:k]} - \Phi_{[0:k]}\theta)
\end{equation}

By some well known results\cite{aggarwal_outlier_2017} we have that 
$D_M \sim \chi^2_k$ and we can construct the model goodness of fit test
\begin{equation}
    P(\chi^2_k \leq D_M) < \alpha
\end{equation}
where $\alpha \in (0,1)$ is a user specified bound.
